Eat-Mor-Cow

  • Originality: The originality of my game is primarily the theme. It is uses the Revenge and Chicken storymatic theme for this week. I ended up going back and forth on what exactly I wanted to create, and decided that I would go for a similar yet different game. From the cows, you can see they say "Eat Mor Chiken," (yes the Chick-Fil-a cows), and the premise of the game is that the chicken is mad about the cows alwas pushing to eat him. So, in order to seek the revenge, the Chicken tries to kill the cows so he can say "Eat Mor Cows!"
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about my game technology is that I did a shooting game for the first time. It took me a while to fully understand the controls, and how the code worked, but I eventually got it. A cool setting that is implemented into this is that there are only 5 eggs/bullets allowed in the frame at once. This makes it harder to win the game as you cant really mess up all that much or else you will loose the game.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? I learned alot from this prototype. I learned how to use cursor shooters, how to adjust the physics of my game elements, and how to limit bullets on the screen itself.

  • Player Experience Goals: I want my players to experience a funny experience as well as obtain better precise motor control.
  • Gameplay: The actions that my players can perform is to move back and forth, as well as shoot at a moving target.
  • Strategies: A player strategy I would expect is that they may try to click too fastly, this will only hinder the likelihood of winning the game, but it will also not allow one to shoot more than 5 eggs at a time. This makes the game harder so the players will have to think about how they can get the amount of correct shots in the fast amount of time.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: A paragraph about the world your game is set in and who the characters are. What makes the game world and its occupants unique and interesting? Do the tokens represent something? If the game has a backstory, mention it here. If the game is abstract, then say so. How will the dramatic tension interact with the gameplay tension? The world my game is set in in a field where the chickens and cows may roam. Out in this field, a large battle goes on between the Chickens and the Moo Cows. The Moo Cows are from chick fil a, and they are tired of being eaten at local fast food resturants, so they always push for the chickens to be eaten as a change. So in this story, my chickens are seeking revenge as they want to live too! The dramatic tension will interact with the gameplay tension by making the player want to defend and help the chicken.
  • Target Audience: The target audience I am trying to reach is pretty much anyone that wants to have fun. The audience is of all ages.
  • Play Time: My play time is around 10 seconds. This is a very fast paced game, the game can be reloaded and played many times quickly. Its a game of skill!
